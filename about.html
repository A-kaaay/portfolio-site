The story

I've spent eleven years in marketing across six industries: from South Asia's largest e-commerce platform, to cybersecurity, to industrial B2B SaaS. Different products, different buyers, different sales cycles. The pattern was always the same: walk into a company with no organic channel, no content engine, no inbound pipeline, and build the system from zero.

Most recently, I spent two years as the first inbound marketing hire at Makula, a German industrial B2B SaaS company. No playbook to inherit, no team to hand things off to, just the work: technical SEO, content, backlinks, and eventually restructuring everything again when AI search started changing how buyers actually find vendors. That case study is public because the numbers are mine to show.

I don't run an agency, and I'm not trying to. Every engagement I take on, I do the work myself: no juniors quietly doing the SEO while a strategist takes the call, no account managers relaying updates from someone else's work. If you hire me, I'm the one in your search console.

How I work
  • Delivery
    Personally, every time. No juniors, no handoffs, no account managers. That's also why I only take on a few clients at once, it's a real capacity limit, not a marketing line.
  • Timeline
    Retainers run a three-month minimum. Honest SEO takes that long to show real movement. Anyone promising ranking results in 30 days is measuring something that doesn't matter, or faking something that does.
  • Proof
    I lead with numbers from one job, not a stitched-together highlight reel. The case study is a real, single engagement, from zero to a Fortune 500 customer.
  • Time zones
    Working from Karachi, mostly async: written updates you can read on your schedule, plus calls booked in your working hours. Clients in Europe and North America both get real overlap.
